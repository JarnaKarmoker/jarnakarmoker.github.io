I am an M.S. student in Biological Sciences at Arkansas State University, where I work in Dr. Guolei Zhou’s laboratory. My research focuses on Schwann cell signaling in the peripheral nervous system, with particular interest in how Cyclase-Associated Protein 1 (CAP1) regulates cAMP-dependent pathways involved in adhesion, migration, proliferation, and nerve repair. :contentReference[oaicite:2]{index=2}

My long-term goal is to become a molecular biologist using advanced molecular and cell biology tools to identify therapeutic targets or strategies for serious pathological conditions, including neuropathies and cancer-related processes. :contentReference[oaicite:3]{index=3}

## Research Focus
My M.S. thesis is titled **Investigating CAP1 (Cyclase-Associated Protein 1) functions in Schwann cells and their regulation by cyclic AMP**. My work examines how CAP1 influences Rap1, ERK, and cofilin-related signaling and how cAMP-mediated CAP1 regulation affects Schwann cell biology relevant to peripheral nerve injury repair and regeneration.
