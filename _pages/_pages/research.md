My research focuses on Schwann cell signaling in the peripheral nervous system, with particular interest in how Cyclase-Associated Protein 1 (CAP1) regulates cAMP-dependent signaling pathways that influence adhesion, migration, and proliferation.

## Current Thesis Research
**CAP1 mediates cAMP regulation of adhesion and proliferation of Schwann cells in the peripheral nervous system**

My thesis investigates how CAP1 contributes to Schwann cell behavior and signaling. This work examines the relationship between cAMP signaling, CAP1 phosphoregulation, and downstream pathways including Rap1, FAK, and ERK.

## Research Interests
- Schwann cell biology
- Peripheral nerve regeneration
- cAMP signaling
- Cytoskeletal regulation
- Cell adhesion and migration
- ERK, FAK, and Rap1 signaling

## Experimental Approaches
- Mammalian cell culture
- Stable shRNA knockdown and rescue
- Western blotting
- Rap1 activation assays
- Cell adhesion assays
- Wound-healing migration assays
- MTT and BrdU proliferation assays
- Imaging and quantitative analysis
