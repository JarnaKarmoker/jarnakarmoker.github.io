I am a researcher in cell and molecular biology with a focus on Schwann cell signaling in the peripheral nervous system. My work investigates how Cyclase-Associated Protein 1 (CAP1) regulates cAMP-mediated signaling pathways that control Schwann cell adhesion, migration, and proliferation, with relevance to peripheral nerve injury, regeneration, remyelination, and demyelinating disorders.

I am currently completing my M.S. in Biological Sciences at Arkansas State University, where I conduct research in Dr. Guo-Lei Zhou’s laboratory.

## Research Focus
My thesis work examines CAP1-dependent regulation of cAMP signaling and its downstream effects on Rap1, FAK, and ERK pathways in Schwann cells.

## Technical Expertise
- Mammalian cell culture
- Stable shRNA knockdown and rescue
- Western blotting
- Rap1 activation assays
- Cell adhesion and migration assays
- MTT and BrdU proliferation assays
- Imaging and data analysis
